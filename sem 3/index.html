Eco-Friendly Educational Chart

B.Ed. Third Semester Creative Project

Eco-Friendly Educational Chart: Advanced Low-Cost Innovation Using Newspaper and Homemade Glue

During our B.Ed. third semester, we continued to refine our innovative teaching aid creation skills by designing advanced eco-friendly educational charts using recycled newspaper and homemade glue. Building on our first semester experience, this project focused on more complex educational concepts and improved presentation techniques. The chart was crafted using old newspapers as the foundation, with flour-and-water based glue, demonstrating our growing expertise in sustainable, cost-effective educational material development. This third semester project showcased our enhanced creativity, improved resourcefulness, and deeper understanding of environmental sustainability in education. These practices continue to prepare us as future educators who can adapt low-cost methods effectively, particularly valuable in resource-constrained educational environments while promoting environmental consciousness among students.

Campus Cleanliness Drive

B.Ed. Third Semester Initiative

Campus Cleanliness Drive by B.Ed. Third Semester Students: Advanced Community Leadership

As seasoned B.Ed. students in our third semester, we took on enhanced leadership roles in organizing and executing a comprehensive cleanliness drive within the campus premises. This advanced initiative demonstrated our growth in community responsibility and our deeper commitment to Swachh Bharat Abhiyan. With increased experience and maturity, we coordinated with junior students and efficiently managed the cleaning of classrooms, corridors, staff rooms, library spaces, and surrounding campus areas. This third semester initiative not only improved the institutional environment but also showcased our development as responsible future educators who can lead by example. The project highlighted our enhanced organizational skills, leadership capabilities, and our evolved understanding of civic responsibility, preparing us to be influential educators who inspire positive change in their communities.
